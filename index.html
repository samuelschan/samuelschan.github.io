Samuel Chan

About CV Think Contact

Samuel Chan is a PhD student and MacCracken Fellow in Music at New York University. His research interests include global music and voice studies, Sinophone studies, and media anthropology. He has presented on musical hatred, vocal failure, and sonic racialization at chapter meetings of the American Musicological Society and the Society for Ethnomusicology; his paper, “Haunting Vocalities: Sinophone Articulations across Asia/America,” was awarded the SEM Hewitt Pantaleoni Prize for the best graduate student paper at the Mid-Atlantic Chapter meeting. He is Associate Editor at SEM Student News and an admin at Decolonizing Ethnomusicology; he has co-organized the sound studies conferences Un/Sounding the Relational City and Sonic Fluidities. He graduated from UC San Diego (MA in Integrative Studies) and the Chinese University of Hong Kong (BA with First Class Honors), where he was awarded the Bernard van Zuiden Music Prize for graduating top of class. His research has been supported by the NYU Asian/Pacific/American Institute, the Hong Kong Jockey Club Music and Dance Fund, the Hong Kong SAR Government, and other grants.
