SAMUEL CHAN

Samuel Chan is a writer and thinking partner in transpacific sound studies, critical university studies, and gastromusicology. He has presented on vocal failure, musical aversion, memes, and digital circulation at Music Archive Finland and the Universities of Michigan, Minnesota, and Hong Kong. With Suzanne Thorpe and Juan David Rubio, he has organized Sonic Fluidities, a sound studies conference featuring keynotes and panels by George Lewis, Clara Latham, Roshanak Kheshti, and David Kirsh. He is Associate Editor at SEM Student News, and moderates Decolonizing Ethnomusicology.

He is a PhD student in Ethnomusicology at New York University. He holds degrees from UC San Diego (MA Integrative Studies) and the Chinese University of Hong Kong (BA Music, First Class Honors), where he was awarded the Bernard van Zuiden Music Prize for graduating top of class. His studies have been fully supported by the Henry MacCracken Fellowship, Hong Kong Jockey Club Music and Dance Fund, Kunkle and Pommerenke Grand Scholarship, and HKSAR Government Scholarship.
