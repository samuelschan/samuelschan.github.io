Samuel Chan

About CV Think Connect

Samuel Chan is a musicologist, baritone, and thinking partner in New York interested in voice studies, Sinophone studies, and media anthropology. He has presented his work on musical hatred, vocal failures, sonic racialization, and digital circulation at chapter meetings of the American Musicological Society and the Society for Ethnomusicology, and conferences at Harvard, Michigan, Minnesota, Helsinki, and Hong Kong. He co-organized Sonic Fluidities and Un/Sounding the Relational City—sound studies conferences featuring George Lewis, Clara Latham, Roshanak Kheshti, and Steven Feld.

Samuel is a PhD student and Henry MacCracken Fellow in Music at New York University. He received his MA in Music (Integrative Studies) from UC San Diego and his BA in Music (First Class Honors) from the Chinese University of Hong Kong, where he was awarded the Bernard van Zuiden Music Prize. His studies has been fully supported by the Hong Kong SAR Government Scholarship, the Hong Kong Jockey Club Music Scholarship, and the Kunkle and Pommerenke Grand Scholarship. He currently serves as Associate Editor at SEM Student News and an admin at Decolonizing Ethnomusicology.
