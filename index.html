SAMUEL CHAN

Samuel Chan is a reader, writer, and thinking partner in New York.

With interests in sonic failure, musical aversion, and anecdotes, he has presented his work on the politics of listening, digital circulation, memes, and queer voices in Helskinki, Minneapolis, Ann Arbor, and Hong Kong. In San Diego, he curated Sonic Fluidities, a sound studies conference featuring keynotes by George Lewis and Clara Latham, a panel with Roshanak Kheshti and David Kirsh, as well as thirty installations, performances, and papers. He is involved in Decolonizing Ethnomusicology and the Student Union Blog at the Society for Ethnomusicology.

He is pursuing a PhD at New York University as a MacCracken Fellow. With full support by the Hong Kong Jockey Club Music Scholarship, Kunkle and Pommerenke Grand Scholarship, and HKSAR Government Scholarship, he has graduated from UC San Diego (MA, Integrative Studies) and the Chinese University of Hong Kong (BA, Music with First Class Honors), where he was awarded the Bernard van Zuiden Music Prize for graduating top of class.
