About CV Think Contact

Samuel Chan is a PhD student and MacCracken Fellow in Music at New York University, whose research interests include global music studies, Sinophone studies, voice studies, and media studies. He has presented on musical hatred, vocal failure, sonic racialization, and digital media in global Asia at chapter meetings of the American Musicological Society and the Society for Ethnomusicology, and at conferences in Finland, Hong Kong, and around the US. Apart from serving as Associate Editor at SEM Student News, the Musicology Communications & Outreach Intern at AMS, and an admin at Decolonizing Ethnomusicology, he also co-organized the sound studies conferences Un/Sounding the Relational City and Sonic Fluidities. He graduated from UC San Diego (MA in Integrative Studies) and the Chinese University of Hong Kong (BA with First Class Honors), where he was awarded the Bernard van Zuiden Music Prize for graduating top of class. His work has been supported by the NYU Asian/Pacific/American Institute, Hong Kong Jockey Club Music and Dance Fund, HKSAR Government Scholarship, and Kunkle and Pommerenke Grand Scholarship, among other awards.
